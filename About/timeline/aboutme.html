1993

Birth.

September, 1993

I was born in Los Venados, a public hospital in the heart of Mexico City.
Though there are few places not considered the 'heart' of the City...


2006

Go North, young man?

December 2006

My dad gets a job at the Mayo Clinic in Rochester, MN. The Reyes family finds out temperatures below zero are a real thing, as is the English language.


2008

I enter High School!

September, 2008

This period was mostly uneventful. Among the things I learned were: English, the word 'speculation' can win debates, cafeteria food is a mystery to those who eat and 'make' it, and all the American sitcoms I watched in Mexico were lying.
No thought about Computer Science entered my mind.


2010

Astonishment, admission, adventure

December 9 - December 20, 2010

After a brief scare in which my family considered moving back to Mexico in my senior year, I applied and was admitted (!!) to Columbia University. Soon after, my family and I drove across the country to settle in Arizona, where my dad was offered a job at Arizona State University.
And yes, this was all part of our plan to experience the most extreme weather the United States could throw at us.


2011

To spectate or not to spectate

November, 2011

I attended an open house event for the Columbia Daily Spectator. Having had experience only in Opinion, I became a columnist and wrote about whatever struck my fancy. Forgetting, of course, that most people do not care about such a fancy...


2012

Have you tried turning it on and off?

October, 2012

This was possibly one of the most momentous decisions of my life. I decided to finally devote all my energy to securing my future as a journalist, and thankfully, happened to find I loved computer science.
I took my first course in Java in the fall, and started to learn how to build a website during Winter Break. Because why not?


Deputy? Not the kind you're thinking of

December, 2012

After much angst and deliberation, I decided to become deputy News editor, a position notorious for the amount of time it takes. It also happens to be, in my opinion, one of the most important at the Spectator, since deputies pitch and direct the content of the paper from day to day. I was beyond excited (and a little terrified) when I was selected.
I can now say it was the best decision I could have made
